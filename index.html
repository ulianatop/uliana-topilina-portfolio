About me

Learning software development with a people-focused mindset

I am a software engineering student at Lake Washington Institute of Technology with a focus on full-stack web development. Through my coursework and projects, I am building experience with Java, JavaScript, SQL, Node.js, HTML/CSS, and database design.

Before studying software engineering, I worked in pharmacy and ran a self-employed wellness business for infants and children. Those experiences helped me develop patience, responsibility, attention to detail, communication skills, and a people-focused mindset that I would love to bring into tech.

I am interested in learning how technology can be used to solve real problems and make everyday tasks easier for users. My long-term goal is to grow as a full-stack developer and eventually move toward product management, especially in areas connected to health technology, AI, and user-centered products.

Outside of school, I enjoy traveling, exploring new places, learning about different cultures, and connecting with people from different backgrounds. These experiences help me look beyond my own perspective, stay open-minded, and better understand how people's backgrounds can shape the way they communicate, learn, and use technology. This is something I want to keep in mind as I continue learning and growing in software development.
